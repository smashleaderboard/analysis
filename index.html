Scoring System

Group stage win: 1 pt each (max 3) R32 win: +3 pts R16 win: +5 pts QF win: +7 pts SF win: +9 pts Final win: +11 pts

Cumulative totals by exit: GS exit = 0–3 pts · R32 exit = 3–6 pts · R16 exit = 6–11 pts · QF exit = 11–18 pts · SF exit = 18–27 pts · Tournament winner = 27–38 pts. Max per player (4 countries all winning everything) = 152 pts.

All player totals are the verified sum of their four countries' individual point estimates. Country points are based on clean exit-round projections (e.g. a team projected to exit at the QF earns: 3 GS wins + R32 win + R16 win + QF win = 3+3+5+7 = 18 pts).

Player-by-Player Analysis

Paul 🐐 — 1st · 45 pts · Win Prob: 24%
Spain (38) + USA (5) + Sweden (1) + Cape Verde (1)

Paul has drawn the golden ticket of this tournament. Spain, co-favourites at +450, are defending champions on form and arguably the most complete squad in the world — Pedri, Yamal, Morata, a settled system under Luis de la Fuente. Projected to win the tournament outright, Spain alone contributes 38 points: 3 group wins + five knockout victories. The USA as co-hosts add a comfortable R32 win (5 pts), while Sweden and Cape Verde contribute modest group stage points. Paul's lead over the field is enormous — he requires Spain to underperform dramatically (e.g. SF exit = only 27 pts) for the chasing pack to catch him. The 🐐 emoji may be self-aware.

Jack — 2nd · 33 pts · Win Prob: 16%
France (27) + Japan (5) + Paraguay (1) + Qatar (0)

France, the world's top-ranked team and joint-favourite at +475, anchor Jack's squad. Projected to reach the semi-final (27 pts), they are a genuine final contender with Mbappé, Griezmann, and a formidable defensive structure. Japan add excellent value with a projected R32 win — they consistently punch above their weight. Paraguay contribute a solitary group win, and Qatar are expected to exit without points. Jack trails Paul by 12 points on current projections, but if France wins the Final and Spain exit early, this race inverts entirely. Jack's realistic best case is ~38 pts if France go all the way.

Oisin — 3rd (tied) · 24 pts · Win Prob: 11%
Portugal (18) + Switzerland (5) + Czechia (1) + Iraq (0)

Oisin's squad is headlined by Portugal, projected to reach the quarter-final for 18 points. Led by Cristiano Ronaldo — potentially his final World Cup — and supported by Bruno Fernandes, Bernardo Silva, and Rúben Dias, Portugal are a genuine QF certainty and SF possibility. Switzerland are a reliable R32 side, defensively organised and capable of an upset. Czechia add a single group win; Iraq contribute nothing. Oisin's total is entirely Portugal-dependent — a semi-final run would push him to 30 pts and into a battle with Jack for second.

Brendan — 3rd (tied) · 24 pts · Win Prob: 11%
Germany (18) + Colombia (5) + Bosnia & Herz. (1) + Panama (0)

Germany anchor Brendan's squad with a projected quarter-final run worth 18 points. Under Julian Nagelsmann with Florian Wirtz and Jamal Musiala now fully mature, Germany are a rebuilt, dangerous side at 14-1. Colombia provide a solid R32 contribution — a capable side at 40-1. Bosnia and Herzegovina add a group win; Panama are expected to exit scoreless. Brendan and Oisin are level on 24 points with an identical structure: one QF-level team, one R32 team, and two group-stage sides. This tie could be broken by a Germany or Portugal semi-final run.

Lochlann — 5th · 21 pts · Win Prob: 9%
Brazil (18) + Uruguay (2) + Australia (1) + Tunisia (0)

Brazil are Lochlann's entire story — projected at the quarter-final for 18 points. Rated +850-900 and one of the most storied nations in World Cup history, Brazil should comfortably reach the knockouts. Uruguay add two group wins; Australia contribute a single win; Tunisia exit without scoring. The squad structure is almost identical to Brendan and Oisin's but falls three points short due to Uruguay and Australia being weaker group contributors than Switzerland or Colombia. A Brazil semi-final run (27 pts) would vault Lochlann above everyone except Paul and potentially Jack.

Conor — 6th · 17 pts · Win Prob: 7%
Norway (10) + Belgium (5) + Turkey (1) + South Africa (1)

Conor has the most intriguing mid-tier squad in the competition. Norway are the dark horse story of 2026 — Erling Haaland leading the line makes them a genuine R16 threat, projected to earn 10 points (2 group wins + R32 win + R16 win). Belgium, potentially in their last hurrah as a generation, add a R32 win for 5 points. Turkey and South Africa contribute single group wins each. If Haaland goes on a run and Norway reach the quarter-final, Conor's Norway total rises to 14, pushing his total to 21 and into the Lochlann/Brendan/Oisin tier. Norway are arguably undervalued at 35-1.

Alex — 7th · 16 pts · Win Prob: 6%
South Korea (5) + Croatia (5) + Senegal (5) + Saudi Arabia (1)

Alex has the most balanced squad in the competition — three countries each projected to earn identical R32 exits worth 5 points apiece. South Korea are a perennial knockout-stage side, Croatia have the experience and nous of a deep generation, and Senegal with Sadio Mané are rated at ~1.5%. Saudi Arabia add a group win. The uniformity is both Alex's strength (no catastrophic zero) and weakness (no tournament-winning anchor to drive a big total). An overperformance by any one of South Korea, Croatia or Senegal — reaching the R16 — would add 5 pts immediately and move Alex into 5th or 6th.

Calla — 8th · 14 pts · Win Prob: 5%
Argentina (11) + Canada (2) + Austria (1) + Uzbekistan (0)

Argentina are the headliner — defending 2022 champions, rated +800-900, and projected to reach the round of 16 for 11 points. Without Messi at peak powers, expectations are managed somewhat, but this remains a quality squad. Canada as co-hosts add two group wins. Austria contribute a single group win. Uzbekistan are expected to exit without scoring. Calla's squad lacks the depth of the tiers above — Argentina are the only meaningful contributor. A deep Argentina run (QF = 18 pts, SF = 27 pts) transforms the picture entirely, as it would for any single-star squad.

Camryn — 9th (tied) · 13 pts · Win Prob: 4%
England (11) + Iran (1) + Algeria (1) + Jordan (0)

England are rated third in world odds at +650-700 and projected to reach the round of 16 for 11 points — Bellingham, Foden, Saka, Kane representing arguably the most talented English squad in decades. Iran and Algeria contribute solitary group wins; Jordan exit without scoring. The irony of Camryn's position is stark: England at +650 should comfortably anchor a top-five squad, but their notorious tournament underperformance keeps projected expectations modest. If England finally deliver and win the tournament, Camryn's total leaps to 38 pts — enough to challenge Paul for first. Everything depends on England being England, or not being England.

Aodhan — 9th (tied) · 13 pts · Win Prob: 4%
Netherlands (6) + Mexico (5) + Scotland (1) + Egypt (1)

The Netherlands are Aodhan's anchor — rated at 20-1, featuring van Dijk, Xavi Simons and a solid core — but are only projected to exit at the R32, giving 6 points. Mexico as co-hosts earn a R32 exit for 5 points, motivated by a raucous home crowd. Scotland and Egypt add single group wins each. Aodhan's total suffered most from the original calculation error in the file — the corrected figure of 13 pts reflects a squad without a QF-level team. A Netherlands or Mexico deep run is needed to change his standings. Netherlands reaching the QF (18 pts) alone would push Aodhan to 30 pts.

Seán — 11th · 10 pts · Win Prob: 2%
Morocco (5) + Ecuador (2) + Ivory Coast (2) + DR Congo (1)

Morocco are the star of Seán's squad — their historic 2022 semi-final run elevated expectations, and they remain Africa's strongest side. However, they are projected only to the R32 this time (5 pts), rated at ~1.5%. Ecuador and Ivory Coast each collect two group wins, DR Congo manage one. There is no route to the top of the standings without Morocco replicating 2022 magic — a semi-final run (27 pts) would transform Seán's total to 32 pts and flip this analysis entirely. Of any single upset scenario in this competition, a Morocco deep run is perhaps the most plausible long shot.

Derek — 12th · 1 pt · Win Prob: 1%
Ghana (1) + Haiti (0) + New Zealand (0) + Curaçao (0)

Derek has the most challenging hand in this competition. Ghana are the only country projected to score — a single group win for 1 point. Haiti, New Zealand, and Curaçao are all expected to exit the group stage without winning a game. Curaçao, competing at their very first World Cup, are a genuinely historic story for the island nation — but not one that scores fantasy points. To have any realistic chance, Derek would need Ghana to go on a historic run, plus multiple other upsets. It is not impossible, but the mathematics are stark: even Ghana reaching the R32 would only add 5 pts, leaving Derek at 6 total — still last.

Methodology

All player totals are the verified arithmetic sum of their four countries' individual point estimates. Country points are based on clean exit-round projections derived from pre-tournament betting odds (BetMGM, FanDuel, Paddy Power), FIFA rankings, and historical tournament performance. Points are cumulative: a country that exits at the quarter-final earns all points from every round it won up to and including that win (3 GS wins + R32 win + R16 win + QF win = 3+3+5+7 = 18 pts). Group stage wins are estimated at 3 for top-10 ranked nations, 2 for solid mid-tier sides, 1 for weaker teams, and 0 for the weakest. The file was fully audited and corrected — all totals have been independently verified.
