Key Insights

🥇 Paul 🐐 is the narrow favourite. Spain are the single most likely World Cup winner according to all major forecasters (Opta: 16.1%, betting markets ~17%), and Paul holds them alongside Sweden (solid R16 prospects), USA (host nation, likely QF run) and Cape Verde (group exit). Spain alone could deliver 38 points if they go all the way.
🎯 Jack has the most balanced hand. France (co-favourite, ~17% to win), Japan (R16 threat), Paraguay (group exit likely) and Qatar (group exit). France could single-handedly outscore most other players' full portfolios.
🌙 Oisin is the sleeper. Portugal at +900 is legitimate value. Switzerland and Czechia are quiet over-performers who could pile on points. Oisin's ceiling is underrated by the field.
🇦🇷 Calla's Argentina factor. Argentina made the final in 18.1% of Opta's simulations — higher than their win probability suggests. If Messi's farewell tour goes all the way, Calla becomes a genuine threat.
⚡ Seán is the dark horse. DR Congo (surprise packet), Ivory Coast (solid African side), Ecuador (strong defensive record, could reach QF) and Morocco (2022 semi-finalists, tipped by some analysts to top Group C). A good run from Morocco and Ecuador could see Seán surge.
💀 Derek faces the toughest draw. Ghana, Haiti, Curaçao and New Zealand are four of the weakest nations in the tournament. All four are expected to exit at the group stage with minimal wins. Derek is almost certain to finish last.
🔥 The Spain factor. Whoever holds Spain (Paul) holds the tournament favourite. Spain won Euro 2024 with Lamine Yamal and Pedri in devastating form. Opta's supercomputer gives them the highest probability of any single nation lifting the trophy.
📊 Format note. The expanded 48-team format means 32 teams advance from the group stage — more than ever before. This makes the Round of 32 a new scoring opportunity not seen in previous sweepstakes. Group-stage wins (1 pt each) can meaningfully accumulate for strong nations playing three games.

Methodology

Projected points are estimated using consensus predictions from Opta's 25,000-simulation supercomputer, FanDuel/DraftKings betting markets, and expert analysis from ESPN, Yahoo Sports, RotoWire and CBS Sports. For each country, the most likely exit round was identified and group-stage wins were estimated based on each team's strength relative to their group opponents. Points are the sum of estimated group wins plus the points awarded for reaching and exiting each knockout round.

Win probabilities for each sweepstake participant are modelled across each country's range of possible outcomes — not just the most likely scenario — weighted by tournament probability.

* This is a pre-tournament projection for entertainment purposes. Football is wonderfully unpredictable — upsets, injuries and VAR controversies may render all of the above completely wrong.
